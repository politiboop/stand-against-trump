## What a Jury Found

### E. Jean Carroll: Liable for Sexual Abuse

In May 2023, a federal jury in the Southern District of New York found Trump **liable for sexually abusing** journalist E. Jean Carroll in a Bergdorf Goodman dressing room in the spring of 1996.

The jury did not find liability for "rape" under New York's narrow legal definition, which requires penile penetration. Judge Lewis Kaplan subsequently clarified this distinction in a July 2023 ruling: "The finding that Ms. Carroll failed to prove that she was 'raped' within the meaning of the New York Penal Law does not mean that she failed to prove that Mr. Trump 'raped' her as many people commonly understand the word 'rape.'" The jury awarded Carroll $5 million.

Trump continued defaming Carroll after the verdict. A second jury awarded her **$83.3 million** in January 2024 — $18.3 million compensatory, $65 million punitive. Total judgments: $88.3 million.

The Second Circuit upheld the first verdict in December 2024. Trump is appealing the second.

Trump's response to the allegations included: "She's not my type" — despite a photograph existing of them together at a party.

## What He Said on Tape

### The Access Hollywood Recording (2005)

Recorded in September 2005, released by the Washington Post on October 7, 2016. Trump speaking to Access Hollywood host Billy Bush on a bus:

> "I moved on her, and I failed. I'll admit it. I did try and fuck her. She was married."

> "I moved on her like a bitch."

> "I just start kissing them. It's like a magnet. Just kiss. I don't even wait."

> "And when you're a star, they let you do it. You can do anything."

> "Grab 'em by the pussy. You can do anything."

Trump initially called it "locker room banter" and apologized. He was elected president one month later. Billy Bush was fired from NBC's Today show within days.

### Walking Into Dressing Rooms (Howard Stern, 2005)

On the Howard Stern Show, Trump described walking into beauty pageant dressing rooms while contestants were undressing:

> "I'll go backstage and everyone's getting dressed, and everything else, and you know, no men are anywhere, and I'm allowed to go in, because I'm the owner of the pageant and therefore I'm inspecting it. You know, I'm inspecting because I want to make sure that everything is good."

> "You know, the dresses. 'Is everyone OK?' You know, they're standing there with no clothes. 'Is everybody OK?' And you see these incredible looking women, and so, I sort of get away with things like that."

This applied to **Miss Teen USA** contestants as well. Four named contestants from the 1997 pageant told BuzzFeed News that Trump walked into the dressing room while girls — some as young as 15 — were changing. Former Miss Vermont Teen USA Mariah Billado recalled Trump saying: "Don't worry, ladies, I've seen it all before."

Former Miss Arizona Tasha Dixon (Miss USA 2001): "He just came strolling right in. There was no second to put a robe on or any sort of clothing or anything. Some girls were topless. Other girls were naked."

## What 26+ Women Have Said

Twenty-six or more women have publicly accused Trump of sexual misconduct spanning from the early 1980s through at least 2016. The accusers largely did not know each other. They describe the same behaviors — kissing without consent, groping, forced touching — which match Trump's own description on the Access Hollywood tape: *"I just start kissing them. I don't even wait."*

### Groping and forced touching

- **Jessica Leeds** (early 1980s): Trump grabbed her breasts and tried to put his hand up her skirt on a first-class airplane flight. Trump's response: "She would not be my first choice, that I can tell you." (NYT, 2016)
- **Kristin Anderson** (early 1990s): Trump slid his hand up her skirt and touched her vagina through her underwear at a Manhattan nightclub. (Washington Post, 2016)
- **Mindy McGillivray** (2003): Trump groped her at Mar-a-Lago during a Ray Charles concert. (Palm Beach Post, 2016)
- **Karena Virginia** (1998): Trump grabbed her arm and touched her breast at the U.S. Open. He said, "Don't you know who I am?" (Press conference, 2016)
- **Cassandra Searles** (2013): Trump "continually grabbed her ass" and invited her to his hotel room. She was Miss Washington USA. Other contestants corroborated. (Yahoo News)

### Kissing without consent

- **Temple Taggart** (1997): Trump kissed her on the lips without consent when she was 21, at a pageant event. (NYT, 2016)
- **Rachel Crooks** (2005): Trump kissed her on the mouth without consent outside Trump Tower elevators. She was 22, a receptionist. (NYT, 2016)
- **Natasha Stoynoff** (2005): Trump forcibly kissed her while she was interviewing him for People Magazine at Mar-a-Lago. Trump's response: "Look at her, I don't think so." (People, 2016)
- **Cathy Heller** (1997): Trump grabbed her and kissed her at a Mother's Day brunch at Mar-a-Lago. (Guardian, 2016)
- **Alva Johnson** (2016): Trump forcibly kissed her at a campaign event. She was a campaign staffer. (Lawsuit, 2019)

### More severe allegations

- **Jill Harth** (1997): Trump forcibly groped her under her dress at Mar-a-Lago. She filed a lawsuit alleging attempted rape. The case settled. (1997 lawsuit)
- **Summer Zervos** (2007): Former Apprentice contestant. Trump kissed her, groped her breasts, and thrust his genitals against her. She sued for defamation after he denied it. (Defamation lawsuit)
- **Jessica Drake** (2006): Trump grabbed and kissed her without consent, then offered her $10,000 to come to his hotel room. (Press conference, 2016)
- **Amy Dorris** (1997): Trump forcibly kissed her and groped her at the U.S. Open. She told friends at the time. (Guardian, 2020, with corroborating witnesses)

## Sworn Testimony: Ivana Trump

During their 1989 divorce proceedings, Ivana Trump testified under oath that Donald Trump **"raped"** her. According to the deposition and later documented in Harry Hurt III's 1993 book *Lost Tycoon*, the incident followed a painful scalp reduction surgery — Trump was angry about the pain from a procedure Ivana's doctor had recommended. He allegedly pulled out fistfuls of her hair and forced himself on her sexually.

In 1993, through her lawyers, Ivana issued a statement: "I referred to this as a 'rape,' but I do not want my words to be interpreted in a literal or criminal sense." She said she felt "violated" but did not want to characterize it as a crime. She later endorsed Trump for president.

The original testimony was sworn under oath. New York criminalized marital rape in 1984 — five years before the alleged incident. Ivana's later walk-back came while she was bound by a confidentiality agreement from the divorce settlement.

## The Epstein Connection

The Epstein relationship is documented extensively in the companion essay. The sexual predator angle:

- **Virginia Giuffre** — one of Epstein's most prominent accusers — was recruited by Ghislaine Maxwell while working as a spa attendant at **Mar-a-Lago** in 2000. She was 16.
- Trump acknowledged Epstein's interest in young women in 2002: "many of them are on the younger side."
- The FBI conducted four interviews in 2019 with a woman who accused Trump of sexually assaulting her at approximately **age 13** in the early 1980s. FBI summary: Trump "forced her head down to his exposed penis." The accuser told the FBI Trump said: **"Let me teach you how little girls are supposed to be."**
- The Post and Courier independently verified multiple biographical details from the accuser's testimony in March 2026, though the assault itself has not been independently corroborated.
- Trump's DOJ withheld 50+ pages of these FBI interview records from the mandated Epstein file release — releasing only the interview about Epstein while withholding the three summaries about Trump.

**This is alleged, not proven.** It is included because the FBI considered it credible enough for four interviews over three months, and because the DOJ's suppression of specifically these documents raises questions about why.

He walked into dressing rooms where teenage girls were changing and bragged about it on the radio. He sexualized his own infant daughter on television and said he'd date a 10-year-old.

The evidence is not ambiguous. It spans four decades, multiple categories of proof, two jury verdicts, and Trump's own recorded admissions.
