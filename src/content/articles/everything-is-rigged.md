Trump has described himself this way: "If it's bad, I say it's fake. If it's good, I say that's the most accurate poll ever."

That's not an opponent's characterization. He said it about polls, but it applies to everything. Awards he didn't win were rigged. Judges who ruled against him were biased. Elections he lost were stolen. An election he won was also stolen — because he didn't win it by enough.

This pattern didn't start with politics. It goes back decades, long before he ever ran for office. And it hasn't stopped for a single year.

## Before Politics

### The NFL (1984-2014)

In 1984, Trump bought the New Jersey Generals of the United States Football League and pushed the league into a direct confrontation with the NFL. When the NFL refused to accommodate a merger, Trump orchestrated a $1.69 billion antitrust lawsuit claiming the NFL held an illegal monopoly that was rigged against the USFL.

The jury found monopolistic behavior — then awarded the USFL exactly $1. Tripled under antitrust law to $3. The league folded six days later.

Trump spent the next three decades trying to buy his way into the NFL. He bid on the Baltimore Colts, the New England Patriots, and the Buffalo Bills. Each time he was blocked. NFL Commissioner Pete Rozelle reportedly told Trump he would never be allowed to buy a franchise as long as Rozelle or his successors ran the league.

After losing the 2014 bid for the Buffalo Bills, Trump claimed he had only bid "to make sure they stayed in Buffalo." Stephen A. Smith later reported that Trump told him: "If these motherf---ers get in my way, I'm gonna get them all back. I'm gonna run for president."

### The Emmys (2004-2016)

The Apprentice was nominated for Emmy Awards multiple times between 2004 and 2009. It never won. Trump spent years calling the awards rigged:

- **September 2012:** "The Emmys are all politics, that's why, despite nominations, The Apprentice never won — even though it should have many times over."
- **January 2013:** "According to many, and while nominated, I would have won the Emmy many times except for my politics."
- **April 2013:** "I should have many Emmys for The Apprentice if the process were fair."
- **An Apprentice episode (January 2015):** "I got screwed out of an Emmy."

During the final presidential debate in October 2016, Hillary Clinton brought up the Emmy complaints. Trump interrupted: "Should have gotten it." Clinton replied: "This is a man who thinks the Emmys were rigged."

The Television Academy responded: "The results of the Emmy Awards are not rigged. Ballots are tabulated by the independent accounting firm of Ernst & Young."

### The Oscars

In March 2014, Trump tweeted: "Which is worse and which is more dishonest — the #Oscars or the Emmys?"

In April 2021, he called the ceremony "a really bad politically correct show" that was "disjointed, boring, and very unfair."

### Forbes 400 (1982-2023)

Trump's relationship with the Forbes 400 list is a case study in the pattern. Forbes reporter Jonathan Greenberg later revealed that Trump "conned his way" onto the inaugural 1982 list by impersonating a spokesman named "John Barron" to inflate his net worth.

When Forbes dropped Trump from the list in 2023, he called it a "Fake Forbes 400 list" and attacked the magazine: "For years Forbes has attacked me with really dumb writers assigned to hit me hard." He also falsely claimed Forbes was "owned by the communist Chinese Government."

### Trump University — Judge Curiel (2016)

When Judge Gonzalo Curiel presided over fraud lawsuits against Trump University, Trump attacked the judge's impartiality based on his ethnicity. "He's a Mexican," Trump said. Curiel was born in Indiana.

Even Paul Ryan called it "the textbook definition of a racist comment."

Trump settled all three cases for $25 million.

### Golf

Rick Reilly's 2019 book "Commander in Cheat: How Golf Explains Trump" documented Trump's golf behavior based on interviews with over 100 golf pros, caddies, and playing partners. Trump claims 18 club championships. Reilly found 16 were fabricated. Caddies described Trump moving his ball, lying about scores, and once throwing broadcaster Mike Tirico's ball off the green.

The golf pattern is a useful lens. Trump doesn't just claim he was cheated out of victories. He cheats to create them, then insists they're real.

### The Miss USA Pageant (2012)

This one is revealing for the opposite reason. When Miss Pennsylvania Sheena Monnin accused the Miss USA pageant — which Trump owned — of being rigged, Trump didn't shrug it off. He sued her for defamation and won $5 million. He called her claims "trashy" and "loser's remorse."

Trump has sued others for $5 million for calling something rigged. He has called approximately 51 things rigged himself.

## 2012: Someone Else's Election

Trump wasn't on the ballot in 2012. Barack Obama beat Mitt Romney decisively. Trump's reaction on election night:

- "This election is a total sham and a travesty. We are not a democracy!"
- "We can't let this happen. We should march on Washington and stop this travesty."
- He called the Electoral College "a disaster for a democracy."

Four years later, after the Electoral College helped him win despite losing the popular vote, he called it "actually genius."

## 2016: The Primary

### Iowa

After losing the Iowa caucuses to Ted Cruz on February 3, 2016:

- "Ted Cruz didn't win Iowa, he stole it. That is why all of the polls were so wrong and why he got far more votes than anticipated. Bad!"
- "Based on the fraud committed by Senator Ted Cruz during the Iowa Caucus, either a new election should take place or Cruz results nullified."

He initially tweeted that Cruz "illegally stole" Iowa, then deleted it and reposted without the word "illegally."

Cruz's campaign had told caucusgoers that Ben Carson was dropping out. Carson wasn't. It was misleading, but it wasn't illegal, and there's no evidence it swung the result.

### Colorado

After Ted Cruz swept all 34 Colorado delegates in April 2016, Trump called the system "rigged" and "crooked." He wrote in the Wall Street Journal that Colorado held "an election without voters."

About 60,000 Republicans actually participated. Cruz's campaign simply had better ground organization. Trump's team hired an in-state consultant less than a week before the assembly.

Cruz responded: "Donald, it ain't stealing when the voters vote against you."

## 2016: The General Election

### Before It Happened

Months before any votes were cast:

- **August 1, 2016 (Columbus, Ohio):** "I'm afraid the election is going to be rigged."
- **August 2016 (Pennsylvania rally):** "The only way we can lose, in my opinion — and I really mean this — Pennsylvania, is if cheating goes on."
- **October 2016:** "The election is absolutely being rigged by the dishonest and distorted media pushing Crooked Hillary — but also at many polling places."

At the final debate on October 19, moderator Chris Wallace asked Trump if he would accept the results of the election. Trump said: "I will look at it at the time." No major-party candidate in modern American history had ever refused to commit to accepting the results.

### After He Won

He won. And he still said it was rigged.

On November 27, 2016 — three weeks after his victory — Trump tweeted: "In addition to winning the Electoral College in a landslide, I won the popular vote if you deduct the millions of people who voted illegally."

He lost the popular vote by nearly 3 million. No evidence of widespread illegal voting was ever produced. PolitiFact rated the claim "Pants on Fire."

In January 2017, he told congressional leaders that 3 to 5 million undocumented immigrants had voted. He then created a Presidential Advisory Commission on Election Integrity to prove it. Eight months later, having found no evidence of widespread voter fraud, the commission was quietly dissolved.

## 2017-2019: The First Term

### The Mueller Investigation

Trump tweeted the words "witch hunt" 294 times during his presidency, overwhelmingly about the Mueller investigation.

- **May 2017:** "This is the single greatest witch hunt of a politician in American history."
- **July 2018:** "The Robert Mueller Rigged Witch Hunt, headed now by 17 (increased from 13, including an Obama White House lawyer) Angry Democrats..."
- **August 2018:** "Attorney General Jeff Sessions should stop this Rigged Witch Hunt right now."

When asked by Congress, Mueller testified: "It is not a witch hunt."

### Inauguration Crowd Size

Trump's first full day in office set the template. He sent Press Secretary Sean Spicer to the podium to claim, falsely, that Trump's inauguration had "the largest audience to ever witness an inauguration — period." Trump personally called the National Park Service to demand better photographs.

Government photographers cropped images to hide empty space at Trump's direction. Crowd-size experts estimated Trump drew roughly one-third of Obama's 2009 crowd.

### Google

In August 2018, Trump tweeted: "Google search results for 'Trump News' shows only the viewing/reporting of Fake News Media. In other words, they have it RIGGED." He claimed "96% of results on 'Trump News' are from National Left-Wing Media."

The statistic came from a conservative blog post. Google responded: "Search is not used to set a political agenda."

### Saturday Night Live

Trump called for the FCC to investigate Saturday Night Live for its portrayal of him, tweeting: "Very unfair and should be looked into. THE RIGGED AND CORRUPT MEDIA IS THE ENEMY OF THE PEOPLE!" He also suggested "there must be Collusion with the Democrats."

He was asking a federal agency to investigate a comedy show.

### Time Person of the Year

In December 2015, after Time named Angela Merkel Person of the Year: "I told you @TIME Magazine would never pick me as person of the year despite being the big favorite. They picked person who is ruining Germany."

In November 2017, Trump claimed Time told him he would "probably" be named Person of the Year but that he'd need to do an interview and photo shoot, which he refused. Time's chief content officer said there was "not a speck of truth" to Trump's claim.

### The First Impeachment

After being impeached for pressuring Ukraine, Trump called the proceedings "a sham impeachment — a blatantly partisan attempt to destroy the President." He called it the "Impeachment Hoax."

## 2020: The Big One

### Before the Election

Starting months before Election Day, Trump made over 150 claims about mail-in voting being fraudulent:

- "Mail-In Voting will lead to massive fraud and abuse."
- He claimed foreign countries would print "MILLIONS OF MAIL-IN BALLOTS."
- **August 17, 2020 rally:** "The only way we're going to lose this election is if the election is rigged."

Trump himself voted by mail in Florida.

### Election Night

At 2:30 a.m. on November 4, with millions of votes uncounted:

- "This is a fraud on the American public. This is an embarrassment to our country. We were getting ready to win this election. Frankly, we did win this election."
- "We want all voting to stop."
- "We'll be going to the U.S. Supreme Court."

On November 7, after the networks called the race: "I WON THIS ELECTION, BY A LOT!"

### 62 Lawsuits

Trump and his allies filed 62 lawsuits in nine states and the District of Columbia. He lost 61 of them. The one "win" was a minor procedural ruling in Pennsylvania about the distance poll watchers could stand from counters.

Judges appointed by both parties, including at least nine Trump appointees, rejected his claims:

- **Judge Stephanos Bibas (Trump appointee, 3rd Circuit):** "Calling an election unfair does not make it so. Charges require specific allegations and then proof. We have neither here."
- **Federal judge in Pennsylvania:** The arguments were "strained legal arguments without merit and speculative accusations... unsupported by evidence."
- **Michigan judge:** The evidence was "speculation and conjecture."
- **Wisconsin court:** The claim was "meritless on its face."

### The Georgia Phone Call

On January 2, 2021, in a recorded call with Georgia Secretary of State Brad Raffensperger, Trump said: "I just want to find 11,780 votes."

Biden had won Georgia by 11,779 votes, confirmed by a hand recount and a machine recount. The call was later cited in criminal indictments.

### January 6

At his rally on January 6, 2021:

- "This year they rigged an election. They rigged it like they've never rigged an election before."
- "Make no mistake, this election was stolen from you, from me and from the country."
- "We will never give up, we will never concede. It doesn't happen. You don't concede when there's theft involved."
- "We fight like hell. And if you don't fight like hell, you're not going to have a country anymore."

He was impeached a second time — the first president in history to be impeached twice.

## 2021-2024: Indictments and Trials

After leaving office, Trump posted about or referenced the "rigged" 2020 election 526 times on Truth Social over 456 days, according to Citizens for Responsibility and Ethics in Washington.

### Every Criminal Case Was Rigged

Trump called all four criminal indictments witch hunts:

- **Manhattan (hush money, March 2023):** "This is political persecution and election interference at the highest level in history."
- **Federal documents case (June 2023):** "The Democrats have lied, cheated and stolen in their obsession with trying to get Trump."
- **Federal election case (August 2023):** Called Jack Smith "deranged" and the case "a Partisan, Unconstitutional, Witch Hunt."
- **Georgia RICO (August 2023):** Called DA Fani Willis "the racist and corrupt district attorney." When documents appeared on the court website before the grand jury voted, he said: "Sounds rigged to me!"

He was convicted on all 34 counts in the hush money case. His response: "This was a rigged, disgraceful trial." He called Judge Juan Merchan "a tyrant," "highly conflicted," and "corrupt, biased, and incompetent."

Before the trial, Trump's own lawyer had said: "I have no reason to believe this judge is biased."

### Every Civil Case Was Rigged

**E. Jean Carroll trial (January 2024):** After a jury awarded $83.3 million: "It's a totally rigged deal. This whole thing is rigged." He called Judge Lewis Kaplan "a nasty man" and "a Trump-hating guy." He said: "The whole New York System is RIGGED against me."

**NY civil fraud trial (2023):** He called AG Letitia James's case "a political witch hunt," called the judge "very partisan" and "run by the Democrats." The judge fined Trump $5,000 and then $10,000 for violating gag orders. The final judgment: $354 million. The judge said Trump's frauds "shock the conscience."

### The 2024 Debate

After the September 2024 ABC News debate with Kamala Harris, Trump called it a "rigged deal" and "three to one" — Harris plus two moderators. He said ABC should lose its broadcast license.

Before the earlier CNN debate in June 2024, he predicted a "90% chance" it would be unfair. He was widely seen as winning that debate.

## 2025-2026: Still Going

### The Nobel Prize

Trump has wanted the Nobel Peace Prize for years.

- **2019:** "They won't give me a Nobel Peace Prize because they only give it to liberals."
- **June 2025:** "I should've gotten it four or five times."
- When he didn't win, he texted Norway's Prime Minister: "Considering your Country decided not to give me the Nobel Peace Prize for having stopped 8 Wars PLUS, I no longer feel an obligation to think purely of Peace."
- He then claimed FIFA gave him their peace prize because they "saw that I got screwed by Norway."

### The Polls

As his approval ratings hit second-term lows (32-37%) amid the Iran war and economic concerns, Trump declared the polls rigged:

- "90% of what they say are lies and made up stories, and the polls are rigged, much as the 2020 Presidential Election was rigged."
- He claimed his "real" approval rating was 64%.
- He said pollsters should be investigated for "election fraud."

### The Virginia Redistricting Referendum (April 22, 2026)

Yesterday, Virginia voters approved a referendum to redraw the state's congressional map. The measure passed 51.45% to 48.55%. By the next morning, Trump was on Truth Social:

"A RIGGED ELECTION TOOK PLACE LAST NIGHT IN THE GREAT COMMONWEALTH OF VIRGINIA!"

He added: "All day long Republicans were winning, the Spirit was unbelievable, until the very end when, of course, there was a massive 'Mail In Ballot Drop!'"

Even Virginia Republicans who were furious about the outcome made no effort to question the legitimacy of the result. The votes were counted. The margin was clear. But it wasn't the outcome Trump wanted, so it was rigged.

### 2020, Five Years Later

In March 2025 — five years after the election, with no new evidence — Trump was still going: "What a difference a rigged and crooked election had on our country. And the people who did this to us should go to jail."

## The Pattern

There are two ways to read this list.

One is that Donald Trump is the unluckiest man alive. Every institution he has ever encountered — the NFL, the Television Academy, Ernst & Young, the Forbes editorial staff, federal judges appointed by both parties (including nine he appointed himself), state election officials (including Republicans), the FBI, the Department of Justice, two special counsels, multiple juries, Google's search algorithm, the Norwegian Nobel Committee, Saturday Night Live, and the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences — is biased against him specifically.

The other is that he does this every time he loses. Not sometimes. Every time. For 40 years. Across every domain of his life.

He said it himself: "If it's bad, I say it's fake. If it's good, I say that's the most accurate poll ever."

He wasn't describing a political strategy. He was describing himself.
